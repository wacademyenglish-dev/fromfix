Directive 01

Welcome, Sheriff

This is a Survival Horror class.

Your B1 (Intermediate) student is a new arrival to the town. Focus on building tension. You will teach them the rules of survival before the sun goes down.

Grammar & Lexis Targets:

  • Modals of Obligation: Must, Mustn't, Have to.
  • Survival Phrasal Verbs: Keep out, Break in, Run away, Get out.

The Premise

Classified File: The Township

SUBJECT: Unwilling residents of a mysterious town.

THE TRAP: Anyone who sees the fallen tree and the crows on the road is already trapped. You cannot drive away.

THE THREAT: By day, the town is normal. By night, terrifying creatures emerge from the forest. They look human. They smile. But if you open the door, they will kill you.

Action Lexicon

Phrasal Verbs of Escape

Keep out

To prevent someone/something from entering.

"Nail the windows to keep the monsters out."

Break in

To enter a building by force.

"They will try to break in if you forget the talisman."

Get out

To escape from a closed place.

"We need to get out of this town!"

Give up

To lose hope and stop trying to survive.

"No matter what you see, you mustn't give up."

Post-Screening

Comprehension Check

Q: Why can't the family drive away from the town?

> Because the road goes in a continuous loop. They are trapped.

🗣️ Discussion Prompt:

How would you react if you realized you were driving in circles and couldn't leave a town?

Q: What do the monsters do at the windows at night?

> They whisper, smile, and ask to be let inside.

🗣️ Discussion Prompt:

Why do you think the monsters pretend to be friendly people from the past instead of just attacking?

Document Review

The Whisper

"They whisper to you. They know your name."

Psychological Terror:

Explore why a monster that whispers your name and smiles is infinitely scarier than a monster that just roars and breaks the door.
